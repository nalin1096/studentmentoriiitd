Main Content

1&2

What is the program?

The change from school to college is drastic. The Student-Mentor program is aimed at helping students to resolve the problems which they might face in coping up with these changes. It is also designed to coach first year students in life skills and academic skills, so that they can navigate their way through their B.Tech degree with efortless ease.

Is there a fee to participate in the mentor program?

There is no fee involved in the program.

3&4

Who is a mentor?

Mentors are handpicked 3rd and 4th year students. They guide you through your freshmen year at college, acting as an anchor in times of stress, an older friend who can help you with academic and life skills or just refer you to the best Chinese restraunt in town.

What kind of problems can I approach them for?

A mentee can approach their mentor regarding any problem which affects their life, no matter how trivial it seems.

5&6

Would my mentor discuss my problems with other people?

No, the mentor has to maintain complete confidentiality. No one else besides him would find out about your problems, without your consent.

Would this go on my record?

No. This will not appear on your record.

7&8

Won't people see me as weird hanging out with older mentors?

Not at all. No one is 'perfectly' well-adjusted. We all need help with personal, emotional and academic issues from time to time. Why not seek out that help from mentors and counselors who've 'been there', and have a few answers for us?

I was brought up to be independent. Isn't it weak to run to a mentor with a problem rather than working it out for myself?

True independence implies that I recognize interdependence. A false pride that refuses to recognize that we all depend on each other, on the outside social world, and on Nature, is not true independence. Having the vulnerability to ask for help when one needs it, and receiving it, is part of real strength of character and independence.
